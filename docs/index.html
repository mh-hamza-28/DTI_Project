The Problem We're Solving

Drug-Target Interaction (DTI) prediction is a crucial step in early-stage drug discovery and development. Knowing precisely whether a chemical compound (drug) binds to a specific target protein can significantly accelerate the discovery of novel therapeutics and repurpose existing drugs for new diseases.

Traditionally, wet-lab experiments have been the sole method for verifying these interactions. However, they are immensely costly, highly prone to failure, and time-consuming. Our computational approach leverages Machine Learning to drastically reduce this surface area, acting as a highly accurate filtration step.

Our Methodology

A rigorous end-to-end pipeline handling molecular geometry to statistical learning.

1

Molecular Simplification

We start with SMILES strings (Simplified Molecular Input Line Entry System) representing our drug compounds.

2

Data Preprocessing & Feature Extraction

Using RDKit, we extract structural features of the drug compound such as Molecular Weight, Hydrogen Donors, and Acceptors. For the protein, we extract compositional sequence features (counts of Alanine, Leucine, etc., and total length).

3

Statistical Modeling

Concatenated feature vectors containing both drug and protein attributes are passed into a Logistic Regression and a highly robust Random Forest Classifier, trained on an 80/20 train/test split dataset.

4

Full-Stack Deployment

Finally, everything is seamlessly tied into a Flask Web Application with a premium Vanilla JS UI, allowing researchers to interface with the predictive models effortlessly.
